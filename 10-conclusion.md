If you've read this far, you now have more clarity about your system's risk profile. That clarity is the first step. The second step is deciding what to do with it.

The honest truth about technical debt remediation is that it doesn't happen organically. Left to the pressures of a normal sprint cycle, the urgent always beats the important. New features get prioritized over the infrastructure work that would make those features stable. The debt compounds. Velocity continues to slow.

The teams that successfully climb out of vibe-coded debt do it by treating remediation as a first-class project: with a defined scope, a prioritized backlog, and dedicated time. Not a single heroic sprint, but a sustained commitment to improving the foundation alongside ongoing product development.
